Sanidhya Gupta is a PhD student in Computer Science at Durham University, United Kingdom, working in quantum information and networking. His research focuses on modular fault-tolerant quantum computing, distributed quantum error correction, quantum networking, scheduling and resource allocation for quantum architectures, and measurement-based quantum computation.

He started the PhD in October 2025 under the supervision of Dr. Thirupathaiah Vasantam and Prof. Neil Walton. His current work spans both theoretical and systems-oriented aspects of quantum computing, with particular interest in how architectural constraints, communication delays, and stochastic resource availability shape the performance of distributed quantum systems.

## Current focus

- Boundary-aware scheduling for distributed quantum error correction under entanglement-generation delays
- Shared and centralized magic-state pumping architectures for modular quantum computers
- Measurement-based approaches to stabilizer encoding, delegated quantum computation, and networked quantum protocols
