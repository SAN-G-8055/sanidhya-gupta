This page highlights three current research directions. The descriptions are intentionally concise so the page stays readable and easy to update as projects evolve.

## 1. Boundary-aware scheduling for distributed quantum error correction

This theme studies seam-refresh policies for modular quantum error correction over noisy photonic interconnects. A central question is how scheduling decisions at module boundaries influence logical performance when entanglement generation is imperfect or delayed.

Current directions include:

- Circuit-level simulation in Stim
- Logical-error behavior under entanglement-generation delays
- Scheduling policies that adapt to communication bottlenecks in distributed architectures

## 2. Magic-state pumping architectures for distributed quantum computers

This line of work examines centralized and shared magic-state supply as an architectural primitive for distributed quantum computers. The goal is to understand when shared distillation or pumping resources improve overall throughput and when they create congestion or coordination overhead.

Current directions include:

- Queueing and inventory models for magic-state supply
- Blocking and waiting-time analysis
- Mean-field approximations for large-scale modular systems
- Ongoing collaborative work with Johannes Borregaard

## 3. MBQC, delegated quantum computation, and networked quantum protocols

This theme connects measurement-based quantum computation with secure and network-aware protocol design. It includes both stabilizer-based constructions and broader questions about how computation and communication should be orchestrated across distributed quantum systems.

Current directions include:

- Measurement-based stabilizer encoding
- Secure and noise-aware delegated quantum computation
- Network coding and entanglement distribution
